Estimating the reed at 3+ yards, this holy oblation would constitute a square of nearly fifty miles on every side. From the north side a portion of nearly twenty miles in width, and nearly fifty in length, was appointed for the priests; and in the midst of this portion, the area of the sanctuary, about a mile square, to be enclosed by a wall - 45:1, 45:2. Next to this, on the south, was the Levites' portion, of the same dimensions as that of the priests' - 45:5
and south of this was the portion for the city, of the same length as those of the priests and Levites, but only half the width - 45:6. These three formed the square of 25,000 reeds, or nearly fifty miles; and that set apart for the prince, the breadth of which is not mentioned, extended in length from north to south, along the east and west sides of the square.

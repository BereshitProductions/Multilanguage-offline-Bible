Luke 7
Overview
7:1 Christ finds a greater faith in the centurion, a Gentile than in any of the Jews;
7:10 heals his servant, being absent;
7:11 raises from death the widow's son at Nain;
7:18 answers John's messengers with the declaration of his miracles;
7:24 testifies to the people what opinion he held of John;
7:31 inveighs against the Jews, who with neither the manners of John nor of Jesus could be won;
7:36 and suffering his feet to be washed and anointed by a woman who was a sinner, he shows how he is a friend to sinners, to forgive them their sins, upon their repentance.

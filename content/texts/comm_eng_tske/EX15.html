2 Kings 7:1 - To morrow; Psalm 78:27 - He rained; 105:40 - asked; Ezekiel 20:7 - I am; 35:13 - I have; Matthew 14:20 - were Verse 13the quailsThe Hebrew TransHebrew\ selav, Chaldee selaiv, Syriac and Arabic selwa, is without doubt the quail, so the LXX render it, a large kind of quail. Josephus, ορτυξ, Ethopic, ferferat, and Vulgate, coturnices, quails, with which agree Philo and the Rabbins. The quail is a bird of the gallinaceous kind, somewhat less than a pigeon, but larger than a sparrow. Hasselquist describes the quail of the larger kind as very much resembling the red partridge, but not larger than the turtle dove; found in Judea as well as in the deserts of Arabia Petra and Egypt; and affording a most agreeable and delicate dish. - The natural manna was never found in the desert where this fell where the common manna does fall;

- It is only in the spring time, in March and April, whereas this fell throughout all the months in the year;

- The ordinary manna does not melt in the sun, as this did - Exodus 16:21


- It does not stink and breed worms, as this did, when kept till the morning - 16:20

- The common manna is medicinal and purgative, and cannot be used for food and nutriment, as this was;

- This fell in a double proportion on the sixth day, and not on the sabbath, as it certainly would have done had it fallen naturally;

- It followed them in all their journeys, where ever they pitched their tents; and

- It ceased at the very time of the year when the other falls, namely, in March, when the Israelites were come to Gilgal.

Whatever this substance was, it does not appear to have been common to the wilderness. From Deuteronomy 8:3, 8:16, it is evident that the Israelites never saw it before; and from a pot of it being preserved, it is certain that nothing of the kind ever appeared again.
